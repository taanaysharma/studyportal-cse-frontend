Algorithm Design & Analysis

CSE 221 Core B.Tech · Semester IV · Year II

Prerequisites

Data Structures Basic Programming

Textbooks

  • Fundamentals of Computer Algorithms – Horowitz, Sahni
  • Introduction to Algorithms – Cormen, Rivest

Reference Books

  • Algorithms – Dasgupta, Papadimitriou, Vazirani
  • Data Structures & Algorithms Made Easy – Narasimha
Course Outcomes▼
  • 1Analyze time and space complexity for given algorithms.
  • 2Use various searching, sorting and graph traversal algorithms.
  • 3Apply efficient design techniques: divide & conquer, greedy, and dynamic programming.
  • 4Describe MST algorithms, single-source & all-pair shortest path, and backtracking.
  • 5Classify NP-hard and NP-complete problems and describe their applications.
Course Content▼

1. Fundamentals of Algorithms

Analysis of complexity, recurrence relations, disjoint set structure.

2. Algorithm Design Techniques

Master's Theorem, divide and conquer strategies, computing time complexity.

3. Greedy & Dynamic Programming

Greedy algorithm paradigms and dynamic programming concepts with classic examples.

4. Graph Algorithms

Spanning tree algorithms (Prim, Kruskal), shortest path algorithms (Dijkstra, Floyd-Warshall).

5. Branch & Bound / Backtracking / BFS / DFS

Branch and bound techniques, backtracking algorithms, graph traversal (BFS and DFS).

6. NP-Completeness

Introduction to P, NP, NP-hard and NP-complete problems and their classifications.

Computer System Organization

CSE 222 / CSE 24222 Core B.Tech · Semester IV · Year II

Prerequisites

Digital Circuit Design (CSE-214)

Textbooks

  • Computer Architecture – Morris Mano

Reference Books

  • Computer Organization and Architecture – William Stallings
  • Computer Architecture – Schaum's Outlines
Course Outcomes▼
  • 1Understand physical organization of digital computers including registers, data flow, micro-operations, and control functions.
  • 2Use various instructions from basic computers to write assembly-language programs for data processing tasks.
  • 3Compare performance of digital computers with varying architectural choices.
  • 4Comprehend the organization and implementation of computer memories.
  • 5Gain knowledge of I/O interfacing methods used by digital computers.
Course Content▼

1. Basic Organization & Data Formats

Overview of computer organization and design; Fixed Point and Floating-Point data formats.

2. Central Processor Organization

General Register Organization, Stack Organization, Bus organized memory, Bus structure, Register Transfer Languages, Instruction Types, expanding op-codes, Addressing Modes.

3. Control Unit Organization

Hardwired control & microprogrammed control organization; micro-instruction formats; micro-program sequencer; control memory; horizontal and vertical micro-programming.

4. Arithmetic Processor Design

Fixed-point signed addition/subtraction; multiplication algorithms (Booth's); division algorithm; performance evaluation; AMDHAL's Law; pipeline processing and hazards.

5. Input-Output Organization

Asynchronous data transfer; asynchronous communication interface; program-controlled, interrupt-initiated, and DMA transfer modes.

6. Memory Organization

Memory categorization; horizontal and vertical expansion; cache memory mapping schemes; associative memory hardware organization.

Fundamentals of Entrepreneurship

HUM 251 Core B.Tech · Semester III/IV · Year II Dept. of Humanities & Social Sciences

Prerequisites

Fundamental understanding of entrepreneurship Understanding of industrial laws & regulations

Textbooks

  • Administrative Thinkers – D. Ravindra Prasad et al., Sterling Publishers, 2010
  • Teaching Entrepreneurship – Haidi, Patricia & Brush, Edward Elgar, 2014
  • Public Personnel Management – Norma M. Riccucci, Rutledge, 2017
  • Labour & Industrial Laws – Padhi, PHI Learning, 2017

Reference Books & Journals

  • Personnel Management – Monappa & Saiyadain, McGraw Hills, 2001
  • Entrepreneurship: Theory, Process & Practice – Howard & Donald, 2010
  • The Journal of Entrepreneurship – Sage Publication
  • International Entrepreneurship & Management Journal – Springer
Course Outcomes▼
  • 1Inculcate basic knowledge of Industrial Laws & management principles to handle industrial day-to-day functions with their dimensions.
  • 2Take appropriate decisions to understand industrial laws and administrative principles for smooth functioning of industry and setting up new business.
  • 3Develop business ideas to become successful entrepreneurs and set up new operational business units to address societal needs at national and local level.
Course Content▼

1. Basics of Entrepreneurship

Meaning, nature, scope, importance, evolution, factors influencing entrepreneurship (social, psychological, economical, environmental). Types of entrepreneurs, new generation entrepreneurship (social, tourism, women). Barriers to entrepreneurship.

2. Rural Entrepreneurship

Rural entrepreneurship in India – introduction, meaning, concept, needs, problems, rural industrialization, entrepreneurial development in backward areas, problems and risks.

3. Industrial Laws

Factory Act 1948, Minimum Wages Act 1948, Provident Fund Act 1952, Dispute Act 1947, Contract Labor Act 1970, Compensation Act 1919, Sexual Harassment Act, SEZ, MSME, ISO standards, ISDBI, New Pension Act 2004.

4. Entrepreneurial Motivation

Maslow's Theory, Herzberg's Theory, Douglas McGregor. Organizational Development Theories (Taylor, Follett, Mayo, Weber, Fayol). Power, Accountability, Responsibility, Control, Transparency and Conflict Resolutions.

5. Government Initiatives

ATAL Innovation Mission, SAMRIDH Scheme, Multiplier Grant Scheme (MGS), Start-up India Seed Fund, Single Point Registration Scheme, Stand-up India Scheme, Ministry of Skill Development, Software Technology Parks.

Data Communications

CSE 224 Core B.Tech (CSE) · Semester IV

Subject Coordinators

  • Dr. Mitul Kumar Ahirwal
  • Dr. Surendra Singh
  • Dr. Vaibhav Soni

Textbooks

  • Data Communication and Networking – B A Forouzan
  • Data & Computer Communication – William Stallings
  • Digital Communications – Simon Haykin
  • Principles of Communication Systems – Taub & Schilling

Online Resources

  • Cisco Networking Resources
  • NPTEL: nptel.ac.in/courses/106/105/106105082/

Course Content Overview▼

1. Fundamentals

Introduction to data communication; layered architecture; switching and routing; Internet and internetworking.

2. Signals & Channels

Analog/digital data and signals; signal properties; sampling and data rate; channel bandwidth; channel capacity (Shannon & Nyquist); transmission impairments (attenuation, distortion, noise).

3. Transmission Media

Channel types; modes of communication; serial & parallel transmission; guided media (types & characteristics); unguided media (types & characteristics).

4. Digital & Analog Transmission

Line coding schemes; modulation techniques comparison; multiplexing (introduction, comparison, applications); interfacing to media.

5. Error Detection, Synchronization & Satellite

Error detection and correction schemes; synchronous and asynchronous transmission; satellite communications and schemes.

6. Multimedia Networks & Security

Multimedia networks; performance parameters; audio and video compression (MP3, JPEG); multimedia services; streaming; secured communications; basics of cryptography.
